# Campaign Bible — Lore, Locations & Artifacts

*The Fifth Crusade*

## Artifacts & Relics

- **Radiance** — an intelligent longsword of Iomedae, recovered dimmed from the cult in Book I and awakened to Harlock; effectively an NPC. Its warmth, cooling, or silence tracks Harlock's inner state.
- **Scales of Terendelev** — four silver scales of the fallen dragon Terendelev, each granting a distinct gift (cloud-walking, a veiling false face, a ward, and one that renders a blade good-aligned). Carried by the four heroes since the caverns of Kenabres.
- **Soul Shear** — Staunton Vhane's glaive; slew Lupenor before his resurrection.
- **The Sword of Valor** — the crusade banner reclaimed beneath Drezen; cleanses evil and can summon an angel of Iomedae.
- **Solemn Hour** — Irabeth Tirabade's ancestral longsword, recovered at the Gray Garrison. Irabeth pressed it upon the company rather than keep it; Lupenor bore it for a time, then passed it to Varic when he took up martial training. (Garbled as "Solomar" in early transcripts.)
- **Battle Hymn** — a cold-iron longsword forged to bite the fiends of the Abyss, won from Scorizscar's hoard on the Gray Road; Varic's current blade.
- **Staunton Vhane's Journals** — recovered enemy intelligence on the Corruption Forge and the demonic hierarchy.

## Enemy Intelligence (from Vhane's journals)

- **Aponavisius** — Marilith commander in the demonic hierarchy.
- **Xanthir Vang** — a leader of the Ivory Sanctum.
- The demonic host actively hunts the heretic succubus **Arueshalae**; clues cross-referenced from the Butterfly Cell.

## Locations

### The Worldwound & Mendev

- **Kenabres** — the crusader city that falls at the campaign's opening; its collapse begins Book I. (Home of Aldwin Brightblade.)
- **Nerosyan** — Mendevian city; origin of Tam.
- **The Gray Garrison** — the grey fortress in ruined Kenabres holding the last wardstone fragment; the climax of Book I, where the party gains mythic power.
- **The Defender's Heart** — fortified inn and crusader stronghold in Kenabres; Irabeth's command post.
- **The Black Wing Library** — the crusade's foremost repository on demons and the planes; partly collapsed.
- **Neathholm** — the underground settlement of the mongrelmen, descendants of the corrupted First Crusade.
- **Takira's Redoubt** — the far western ruin where Arueshalae was run to ground (Book II, Ch XVIII–XIX).
- **The Gray Road / the Marchlands** — the broken country crossed on the long march west.

### The Citadel of Drezen

- **The Sally Port** — the initial breach point of the siege.
- **The Watchtower** — outer defensive point, held by Brimoraks and a roaming Chimera.
- **The Murder Room** — a lethal trap-chamber of illusion floors in the upper tiers.
- **The Shrine of the False Goddess** — a corrupted site dismantled early in the infiltration.
- **The East Garrison** — fortified section once held by a Nabasu demon.
- **Corridors of Deceit** — the treacherous middle tier where Kerindra was met and Joran Vhane surrendered.
- **The Ritual Chamber** — dark magical site in the deep dungeons.
- **The Anti-Magic Cell** — a zone that suppresses all magic; site of Chorussina Domal's defeat.
- **The Butterfly Cell** — a holding area (once Arueshalae's) bearing clues to the heretic.
- **The Corruption Forge** — the citadel's defiled heart, guarded by a Shadow Demon; used to taint artifacts and hold prisoners.

## Powers & Factions

- **Deities in play:** Sarenrae (the Dawnflower), Iomedae, Desna.
- **Rabiah's Redeemers** — the party's informal moniker.
- **Iomedae's Preservers** — Harlock's military order holding Drezen.
- **Lupenor's Market** — Lupenor's trading house in Drezen.
- **The Ivory Sanctum** — demonic power center associated with Xanthir Vang.
