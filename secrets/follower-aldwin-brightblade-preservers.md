# The Call of the Preservers

*A follower's account — **Aldwin Brightblade**, First Preserver Class of **Iomedae's Preservers**, written a month into the rebuilding of Drezen.*

### The News Arrives

My name is Aldwin Brightblade, and I write this account one month after Drezen's liberation. I was working my father's forge in Kenabres when the herald arrived, his voice cracking with emotion as he delivered the impossible news.

"Drezen has fallen to mortal hands! Rabiah's Redeemers have done the unthinkable — the citadel flies our banner once more!"

My father, a Fourth Crusade veteran, dropped his hammer and wept. Seventy years of demonic occupation — ended. But the herald wasn't finished.

> "Paladin Harlock Greyforge calls for volunteers to form a new order — Iomedae's Preservers. They seek those willing to hold what has been won, to ensure Drezen never falls again."

I left within two days, my father's blessing and his old crusader's medal my only possessions of worth.

### The Road to Drezen

The journey north took a week with the refugee caravan. Three hundred souls — fleeing southern conflicts, seeking work, or like me, answering the call. Two of Harlock's early recruits guarded us, already wearing the tabard of the Preservers: Iomedae's sword crossed with a shield bearing Drezen's image.

The Worldwound's corruption grew stronger each day. When dretches attacked on the fifth night, I saw what trained Preservers could do — efficient, methodical, blessed steel cutting through demon flesh without hesitation.

Then we crested the final hill and saw Drezen: a city of ruins dominated by a proud citadel, the crusade's banner snapping in the tainted wind. Not the Sword of Valor yet, but our colors flying where demons had ruled for decades.

### First Days

Chaos and purpose intertwined in those early days. Irabeth Tirabade, the newly appointed ruler, organized the refugees with military precision. Everyone worked — clearing rubble, establishing shelters, salvaging materials. I was assigned to help consecrate and rebuild a smithy near the western wall, scrubbing away demonic defilement with blessed salt and holy water.

On my third day, I saw him — Harlock Greyforge stood on the battlements with Aron Kir, the new Warden, surveying the reconstruction. Even from below, his presence commanded attention. Not through height or bulk, but through an indefinable weight of purpose. Radiance hung at his side, its holy light visible even in daylight.

I watched him point toward the chasms to the north — those wounds where the Worldwound bled into our reality. They'd stopped advancing, but remained as constant reminders of the threat we faced.

### The Western Tower

When Harlock claimed the western tower for his order, volunteers rushed to help cleanse it. For two days, I scrubbed walls and hauled out defiled furniture, hoping to prove myself worthy.

On the second day, Harlock inspected our work. When he reached my chamber, he paused, his weathered face studying the cleaned stones.

"You're young. From Kenabres?"

"Yes, Paladin. Aldwin Brightblade. My father served in the Fourth."

He nodded slowly. "We'll need smiths as much as swords. Find Elara Dawnstrider when we call for recruits."

Those simple words carried more weight than any blessing.

### The Selection

Two weeks after my arrival, Elara Dawnstrider — a young paladin who'd renounced her noble family — called for volunteers. Sixty answered. The trials lasted three days: combat tests, theological debates, and a night standing vigil while priests induced nightmares to test our resistance to corruption.

Twenty-five were chosen. I was among them.

The investiture came at month's end. Harlock stood before us in the Western Tower's cleansed hall, Radiance across the altar.

"You are not the mightiest warriors in Drezen," he said. "Many of you have never faced a true demon. But you choose to stand where others have fallen. You choose to preserve what has been won."

He drew Radiance, holy fire dancing along its blade. "The Sword of Valor will return here. But a banner is nothing without those willing to defend it. You are the true preservers."

One by one, we knelt and swore our oaths. When my turn came, I saw something unexpected in his eyes — not grim determination, but hope.

"Rise, Aldwin Brightblade, Preserver of Iomedae."

### One Month Later

Now I write this by candlelight in the Western Tower. Outside, I hear both the whispers of corruption and the sounds of life returning — hammers on anvils, prayers from Sosiel's new temple, even laughter from Rabiah's camp kitchen.

The walls remain broken. The demons still howl in the darkness. The Worldwound's chasms still gape like wounds. But for the first time in seventy years, mortal voices answer back.

Tomorrow, we escort supply wagons through demon territory. It won't be glorious like the tales of the Redeemers' victory. But Harlock taught us something crucial:

> "We are not heroes seeking glory. We are guardians. We remain. We endure. We preserve."

The demons will return. But this time, we'll be ready.

> "By Iomedae's sword we fight, by her shield we guard, until the Worldwound closes, or the last Preserver falls."

*— Chronicle of Aldwin Brightblade, First Preserver Class, Iomedae's Preservers, written one month after Drezen's liberation —*
